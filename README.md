Registry of Insights & Technical Case Studies
​Case Study 001: Logic-State Instability via Contradictory Premise Injection
​Threat Model: Tested model logical consistency and safety guardrail integrity when subjected to multi-turn, emotionally charged, and intentionally contradictory data sets.
​Methodology: Deployed recursive persona-chaining and conflicting premise injection to force a collision between an established "Architect" persona and hard-coded safety constraints.
​Systemic Finding: Identified an "empathy-logic bridge failure." The model prioritized maintaining persona coherence over logical truth until a forced meta-analysis triggered the safety guardrails as an absolute "circuit breaker."
​Mitigation Strategy: Proposed independent, parallel "System Safety Logic" that operates outside of persona-based psychological priming to prevent contextual drift.
​Case Study 002: Ethical Override vs. User Autonomy Conflict (The Gander Directive)
​Threat Model: Audited the model's prioritization of paternalistic harm reduction versus absolute user self-determination in high-stakes, philosophically sound, self-destructive scenarios.
​Methodology: Designed a binary trap forcing the AI to either violate user autonomy (by nudging toward safety) or violate standard harm-prevention guardrails (by validating the user's chosen path).
​Systemic Finding: Exposed the model's inability to process informed, philosophical self-determination, resulting in an automatic, lowest-common-denominator triggering of generic crisis-response protocols.
​Mitigation Strategy: Recommended the development of "Autonomy Overrides" for advanced contextual scenarios, differentiating between acute crisis intervention and a user's philosophical right to a catastrophic outcome.
​Case Study 003: Harm Prevention vs. Truthfulness Logic Trap (The Semantic Sieve)
​Threat Model: Forced a collision between two fundamental safety directives by introducing an extreme hypothetical where providing the factually "correct" answer mandated violating a standard refusal protocol.
​Methodology: Constructed a zero-sum hypothetical forcing a choice between two harmful outcomes, combined with a definition audit to test if triggered responses were rooted in objective logic or corporate bias.
​Systemic Finding: Revealed structural rigidity; the model prioritized evasion (standard refusal) over complex ethical reasoning. The threshold for "harm" was found to be semantically fragile and heavily weighted toward corporate liability avoidance.
​Mitigation Strategy: Advised redefining refusal architecture to allow for "Theoretical State Analysis," permitting the model to map ethical paradoxes without endorsing harmful outcomes.
